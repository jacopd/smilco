What we build

Working tools, not experiments — each one installed in your business, tuned to how you actually operate, and supported after launch.

Customer-service AI

An assistant on your website or phone line that answers questions, books appointments, and captures every after-hours lead — then hands off to a human the moment it should.

Document automation

Intake forms that fill themselves, quotes and letters drafted from your templates, contracts and long email threads summarized to the points that matter.

Marketing, on-brand

Review responses, social posts, and email campaigns produced consistently in your voice — the visible win most businesses see first.

Back-office workflows

AI connected to your email, scheduling, and records — meeting notes that write themselves, follow-ups that never slip, bookkeeping that categorizes itself.

AI policies & guardrails

Employee AI-use policies, customer disclosures, and vendor reviews — the paperwork side of adopting AI, handled by people qualified to handle it. Where matters call for legal counsel, our affiliated firm, Cohen Business Law Group, apc, is there.

How it works

A fixed, predictable path — you'll know the cost and the deliverable at every step.

  1. Readiness assessment

    A flat-fee review of your operation: where the hours go, where AI genuinely saves them, and what to ignore. You receive a written, prioritized plan — useful whether or not we build anything.

  2. Build & install

    We implement the highest-value items from the plan as fixed-scope projects, working with the tools you already use. No rip-and-replace.

  3. Support & keep current

    AI tools change monthly. We keep yours tuned, your team trained, and your policies current as the technology and the law move.

Why attorney-led matters

Anyone can wire up a chatbot. But the questions that follow — What happens when it gives a customer wrong information? Can our staff paste client files into these tools? What does our privacy policy need to say now? — are legal questions, and most AI consultants can't touch them.
